# Service Definition: Human Resources Service

## Service Overview

- **Service Name:** HR
- **Service Version:** 1.0
- **Internal Name:** hr-services
- **Service Type:** Employee Lifecycle Management
- **Status:** Active
- **Provider Organization:** Human Resources Department, Springfield Municipality
- **Service Owner:** HR Manager

## Service Description

The HR Service provides comprehensive human resources management for Springfield Municipality, encompassing the complete employee lifecycle from recruitment through retirement. The service delivers employee onboarding, benefits administration, payroll coordination, performance management, training and development, employee relations, and compliance with federal, state, and local employment regulations.

This service ensures that Springfield Municipality attracts, develops, and retains a high-quality workforce capable of delivering excellent public services to the community. The HR Service acts as a strategic partner to all municipal departments, providing expertise in workforce planning, organizational development, and employee experience management.

The service supports approximately 500+ municipal employees across multiple departments including Public Safety, Public Works, Parks & Recreation, Finance, Community Development, and Administrative Services.

## Service Customers

### Primary Customers

- **New employees**
  - Type: Internal
  - Description: Newly hired municipal employees requiring comprehensive onboarding services, including orientation, training, benefits enrollment, and integration into the organization. This includes full-time, part-time, seasonal, and temporary employees across all departments and position levels.

- **Department managers**
  - Type: Internal
  - Description: Hiring managers and department heads who receive new employees into their teams. They require HR support for recruitment, onboarding coordination, performance management, employee relations, and workforce planning. They are both customers and partners in service delivery.

### Secondary Customers

- **All Current Employees:** Ongoing HR services including benefits administration, payroll, employee relations, training, and career development
- **Department Heads:** Strategic workforce planning, organizational development, succession planning
- **City Leadership:** Compliance reporting, workforce analytics, policy development
- **Job Candidates:** Recruitment and hiring process support
- **Retirees:** Retirement benefits coordination and transition support

### Customer Needs

**New Employees Need:**
- Clear, timely communication about start date, first day logistics, and expectations
- Efficient, user-friendly paperwork completion process
- Warm welcome and feeling of belonging from day one
- Comprehensive understanding of role, responsibilities, and performance expectations
- Knowledge of organizational culture, values, and operating procedures
- Access to necessary systems, tools, and workspace to perform job
- Understanding of benefits options and support in making informed enrollment decisions
- Connections with colleagues, supervisor, and broader team
- Clarity on career development opportunities and pathways
- Confidence and competence to successfully perform job duties

**Department Managers Need:**
- Seamless coordination of pre-boarding activities (IT, facilities, equipment)
- New employees who are properly prepared, trained, and equipped on day one
- Support in delivering effective onboarding and training
- New employees who integrate successfully and reach productivity quickly
- Assistance with performance management and employee development
- Partnership in addressing employee relations issues
- Efficient HR processes that don't burden supervisors unnecessarily

## Service Experience

### Service Ambition

New employees feel welcomed, informed, and equipped to contribute effectively from day one. They understand municipal values, have clear role expectations, and feel connected to their team and the broader organization.

The HR Service aspires to create a world-class employee experience where every new hire transitions smoothly into their role, develops strong relationships with colleagues, gains deep understanding of Springfield Municipality's mission and values, and builds confidence and competence rapidly. New employees should feel that joining Springfield Municipality was an excellent career decision and that they are valued members of a professional, supportive organization dedicated to public service excellence.

Department managers experience the HR Service as a trusted, responsive partner that makes hiring and onboarding effortless, delivers well-prepared new employees, and provides expert guidance on all people-related matters. Managers should feel confident that HR supports their success in building and leading effective teams.

### Experience Management

**Continuous Monitoring:**
- Pulse surveys at 30, 60, and 90 days capture new employee sentiment and experience
- Daily check-ins between supervisors and new employees during first two weeks
- HR Business Partner monitors onboarding progress weekly for all active new hires
- Real-time issue escalation process for problems requiring immediate attention
- Benefits follow-up at 30 days ensures enrollment success
- Anonymous feedback mechanisms enable candid input

**Regular Review Cycles:**
- Monthly HR team review of onboarding metrics, trends, and individual cases

- Quarterly stakeholder meetings with supervisors and department heads to gather input

**Continuous Improvement Process:**
- Feedback from surveys and conversations directly informs process improvements

- Pilot testing of new approaches with small groups before full rollout
- Best practice sharing across departments
- Benchmarking against other municipalities and public sector organizations
- Regular updates to onboarding materials, training content, and technology
- Recognition of supervisors and departments demonstrating onboarding excellence

**Accountability:**
- HR Manager owns overall service quality and experience metrics
- HR Business Partners accountable for individual new hire success
- Department Heads accountable for supervisor adherence to onboarding standards
- Service metrics reported monthly to HR Director and quarterly to City Manager
- Onboarding quality integrated into supervisor performance evaluations

## Service Facilities (Goods & Actions)

### Digital Facilities

#### Employee Self-Service Portal
- **Type:** Goods
- **Functionality:** Online platform for new employees to complete onboarding tasks, access HR information, and submit required documentation. Features include: secure document upload with e-signature capability, real-time onboarding checklist tracking, benefits information and enrollment, policy and handbook access, HR forms library, direct messaging with HR support team, knowledge base and FAQs, employee directory, and learning management system integration.
- **Functioning:**
  - **Availability:** 24/7/365 with 99.5% uptime SLA (planned maintenance windows communicated 1 week in advance)
  - **Performance:** Page load times <2 seconds, mobile-responsive design supporting iOS and Android devices
  - **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliant for accessibility
  - **Security:** HTTPS encryption, multi-factor authentication, role-based access controls, automatic session timeout after 20 minutes of inactivity
  - **Support:** Integrated chat support during business hours, help documentation, video tutorials
  - **Data Management:** Automatic data synchronization with HRIS (BambooHR), document retention per records policy, audit logging of all transactions
- **User Restrictions:**
  - Available to all municipal employees with valid credentials
  - New employees receive portal access via secure email 5 days before start date
  - Access level varies by role (employee view, supervisor view, HR administrator view)
  - External access requires VPN or secure portal login
  - Account deactivated within 24 hours of employment separation

#### HRIS (BambooHR)
- **Type:** Goods
- **Functionality:** Comprehensive human resources information system serving as system of record for all employee data, organizational structure, time-off tracking, performance management, and reporting/analytics.
- **Functioning:** Cloud-based SaaS platform, 99.9% uptime, real-time data updates, integrated with payroll and benefits systems, automated workflows for approvals
- **User Restrictions:** Role-based access for HR staff, supervisors (view/edit direct reports only), employees (view/edit own information only)

#### Time & Attendance System (Kronos)
- **Type:** Goods
- **Functionality:** Electronic timekeeping system for recording hours worked, time-off requests, and timesheet approval workflow.
- **Functioning:** Available 24/7 via web browser and mobile app, integration with payroll system, real-time accrual balance calculations
- **User Restrictions:** All hourly employees required to use, salaried exempt employees use for time-off requests only, supervisor approval required before payroll processing

### Service Actions

#### Onboarding Support and Guidance
- **Type:** Actions
- **Functionality:** Personalized onboarding assistance including orientation sessions, buddy program assignment, HR consultation, and continuous support throughout first 90 days. Includes: welcome and first-day orientation, comprehensive training on HR policies and procedures, benefits enrollment consultation, onboarding buddy matching and coordination, check-in meetings at 30/60/90 days, escalation support for issues, and coaching for supervisors on effective onboarding.
- **Functioning:**
  - **Scheduled Orientation Sessions:** Held twice monthly (2nd and 4th Friday) for group orientation, individual orientation provided on day one for all new hires
  - **Buddy Program:** Onboarding buddy assigned within 3 business days of hire, buddy relationship maintained for first 90 days minimum
  - **HR Consultations:** Available during business hours (8:00 AM - 4:00 PM), appointments scheduled within 24 hours of request, urgent matters addressed same-day

- **User Restrictions:**
  - New employees during first 90 days of employment (probationary period)
  - Extended support available for employees in complex roles or experiencing integration challenges
  - Onboarding buddies are volunteer experienced employees (minimum 1 year tenure) nominated by supervisors and trained by HR
  - Supervisor participation required at key milestones (30/60/90-day reviews)

## Service Routines

### Onboarding Routine

- **Description:** Comprehensive employee onboarding process that ensures new hires successfully transition into Springfield Municipality through structured orientation, training, integration activities, and performance evaluation during the 90-day probationary period. The routine encompasses pre-boarding preparation, first-day welcome, systematic training delivery, relationship building, continuous feedback, and assessment of readiness for permanent employment status.

- **Trigger:** Candidate acceptance of job offer (verbal or written acceptance of offer letter)

- **Frequency:** Continuous process initiated for each new hire, with approximately 2-5 new employees starting monthly across the municipality (15-40 per year organization-wide)

- **Duration:** 90 days from start date (probationary period), with active HR coordination most intensive during first 30 days

- **Participants:**
  - **New Employee:** Completes all onboarding requirements, training, and probationary performance expectations
  - **HR Recruiter:** Manages offer process and background check initiation
  - **HR Coordinator:** Coordinates pre-boarding logistics, facilitates Day 1 orientation, manages onboarding documentation
  - **HR Manager:** Oversees onboarding program quality, conducts welcome, approves critical decisions
  - **HR Business Partner:** Monitors onboarding progress, provides supervisor coaching, ensures successful integration
  - **Benefits Administrator:** Conducts benefits orientation and enrollment meetings
  - **Payroll Specialist:** Sets up payroll, ensures accurate first paycheck
  - **IT Administrator:** Provisions systems access, equipment, and technology training
  - **Facilities Coordinator:** Prepares workspace, issues building access, conducts safety training
  - **Security Officer:** Issues photo ID badge with appropriate access levels
  - **Hiring Supervisor:** Primary onboarding leader, conducts department orientation, delivers job training, provides continuous feedback and evaluation
  - **Department Head:** Approves onboarding plan, welcomes new employee, reviews performance assessments
  - **Onboarding Buddy:** Peer mentor providing informal support, cultural integration, and relationship building
  - **Subject Matter Experts:** Deliver specialized job-specific training
  - **Safety Coordinator:** Provides workplace safety and emergency procedures training
  - **IT Security Officer:** Conducts information security and data privacy training
  - **Ethics Officer (City Attorney):** Oversees ethics and compliance training
  - **DEI Officer:** Delivers diversity, equity, and inclusion training

#### Process Steps Overview

The Onboarding routine consists of 30 major process steps spanning from job offer acceptance through 90-day probationary completion. Detailed step-by-step procedures are documented in the Springfield Municipality Employee Onboarding Process (see reference: `/src/tmp/springfield-onboarding-process.md`).

**Pre-Start Phase (Offer Acceptance to Day 0):**

1. **Job Offer Acceptance and Background Check Initiation** (Days -14 to -7)
   - Responsible: HR Recruiter
   - HR sends official offer letter, initiates comprehensive background screening, creates employee file, communicates tentative start date

2. **Pre-Boarding Preparation** (Days -7 to -5)
   - Responsible: HR Coordinator
   - Coordinates with IT, Facilities, and Department to prepare workspace, technology, access credentials; schedules orientation; assigns employee ID

3. **New Hire Paperwork Portal Completion** (Days -5 to -3)
   - Responsible: New Employee
   - Completes mandatory employment forms online (personal information, emergency contacts, tax withholding, direct deposit, acknowledgments, policy agreements, confidentiality agreements)

**First Day (Day 1):**

4. **First Day - Morning Arrival and Welcome** (Day 1, 8:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
   - Responsible: HR Coordinator
   - Warm greeting, I-9 verification, wet signature collection, HR orientation presentation (municipality overview, policies, payroll, safety, culture), temporary badge issuance, employee photo

5. **First Day - Department Orientation and Workspace Setup** (Day 1, 12:00 PM - 5:00 PM)
   - Responsible: Hiring Supervisor
   - Department welcome, facility tour, team introductions, onboarding buddy introduction, workspace setup, discussion of role and first-week expectations

**First Week (Days 2-5):**

6. **IT Systems Training** (Days 2-3)
   - Responsible: IT Administrator
   - Comprehensive training on email, network drives, intranet, timekeeping, collaboration tools, security practices, remote access, role-specific applications

7. **Benefits Enrollment** (Within first week, by Day 5)
   - Responsible: Benefits Administrator
   - One-on-one consultation reviewing all benefit options (health, dental, vision, life, disability, retirement, FSA, EAP, PTO), enrollment form completion, premium calculation

8. **Safety and Emergency Procedures Training** (Days 3-5)
   - Responsible: Safety Coordinator
   - Emergency procedures, building evacuation routes, workplace safety, incident reporting, security protocols, ergonomics

9. **Payroll and Timekeeping Training** (Days 3-5)
   - Responsible: Payroll Specialist
   - Payroll schedule, time entry procedures, time-off requests, first paycheck timing, pay stub interpretation

10. **Ethics and Compliance Training** (Week 1)
    - Responsible: New Employee (self-paced online)
    - Code of conduct, conflicts of interest, gift ban, confidentiality, whistleblower protections, anti-discrimination, harassment prevention

**Weeks 2-4 (Days 6-20):**

11. **Department-Specific Training** (Weeks 1-2)
    - Responsible: Hiring Supervisor
    - Job-specific processes, procedures, systems, performance expectations, shadowing, initial work assignments with coaching

12. **Facility Access and Security Badge** (Week 1)
    - Responsible: Security Officer
    - Permanent photo ID badge issuance with appropriate building access levels, security policy review

13. **IT Security and Data Privacy Training** (Weeks 2-3)
    - Responsible: IT Security Officer
    - Cybersecurity, phishing awareness, data classification, privacy laws (FOIA, PII), acceptable use policies, incident reporting

14. **Customer Service Excellence Training** (Weeks 2-4, for public-facing positions)
    - Responsible: Customer Service Coordinator
    - Communication skills, handling difficult situations, phone/email etiquette, problem-solving, cultural competency

15. **Financial Systems and Procurement Training** (Weeks 3-4, for positions with financial responsibilities)
    - Responsible: Finance Analyst
    - Financial system navigation, purchase requisitions, procurement policies, expense reimbursement, budget management

16. **Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Training** (Weeks 3-4)
    - Responsible: DEI Officer
    - DEI fundamentals, unconscious bias, inclusive workplace practices, EEO compliance, serving diverse communities, allyship

17. **Emergency Preparedness and Business Continuity** (Week 4)
    - Responsible: Emergency Management Coordinator
    - Emergency operations plan, employee roles during emergencies, continuity of operations, personal preparedness

18. **Department Meeting Introduction** (Within first 30 days, at next scheduled meeting)
    - Responsible: Department Head
    - Formal introduction to entire department team at all-staff meeting

19. **Union Orientation** (Weeks 2-3, if position is union-represented)
    - Responsible: Union Representative
    - Union overview, membership information, collective bargaining agreement, employee rights, union resources

20. **New Employee Orientation Program** (Within first 30 days, monthly group session)
    - Responsible: HR Coordinator
    - Full-day group orientation for all recent hires: organizational overview, culture and values, department spotlights, community overview, employee resources, networking

21. **Community Tour and Municipal Services Overview** (Weeks 4-6, quarterly group tours)
    - Responsible: HR Coordinator
    - Half-day tour of municipal facilities and community to understand services provided and community served

**30-Day Milestone (Day 20-25):**

22. **30-Day Check-In** (End of Week 4)
    - Responsible: Hiring Supervisor
    - Structured review meeting: discuss experience, progress, challenges, feedback on performance, training needs, expectations for next 30 days

23. **Benefits Enrollment Follow-Up** (Day 30)
    - Responsible: Benefits Administrator
    - Confirm receipt of insurance cards, verify enrollment accuracy, provide guidance on using benefits, address issues

**60-Day Milestone (Day 60):**

24. **Probationary Period 60-Day Review** (Day 60, end of Week 8-9)
    - Responsible: Hiring Supervisor
    - Formal performance evaluation assessing job knowledge, quality, communication, teamwork, attendance, professionalism; identify strengths and development areas; create improvement plan if needed

25. **Performance Goal Setting** (Days 60-75)
    - Responsible: Hiring Supervisor
    - Establish 3-5 SMART performance goals and 1-2 professional development goals for remainder of year

26. **Professional Development Planning** (Days 60-75)
    - Responsible: Hiring Supervisor
    - Discuss career interests, identify training opportunities, create Individual Development Plan

**90-Day Milestone (Day 85-90):**

27. **90-Day Probationary Review and Decision** (Days 85-90)
    - Responsible: Hiring Supervisor
    - Comprehensive probationary evaluation, recommendation on employee status (pass, extend, or end probation), final review meeting

28. **Post-Probation Transition to Regular Status** (Day 91)
    - Responsible: HR Coordinator
    - Process status change in HRIS, notify of expanded benefits eligibility, add to annual performance evaluation schedule, send congratulatory communication

**Continuous Activities (Days 1-90):**

29. **Ongoing Integration and Development** (Days 1-90, continuous)
    - Responsible: Hiring Supervisor, HR Business Partner, Onboarding Buddy
    - Weekly one-on-ones, continuous feedback, gradual responsibility increase, relationship building, monitoring, coaching, issue resolution

30. **90-Day Onboarding Completion and Feedback** (Day 90)
    - Responsible: HR Business Partner
    - Comprehensive onboarding survey, feedback debrief conversation, data analysis for continuous improvement, quarterly reporting

## Service Suppliers

### Internal Suppliers

#### Municipal IT Department
- **Type:** Internal
- **Description:** Internal IT department providing systems access, equipment, and technology support
- **Supplies:** Computer hardware (laptops, desktops, monitors, peripherals), software licenses and applications, network access and user accounts, email and collaboration tools, phone/VoIP setup, IT training and documentation, help desk support, cybersecurity training
- **Service Level Agreement:** Equipment provisioned within 5 business days of request, account creation within 2 business days, IT orientation scheduled within first week, help desk response within 4 hours
- **Dependencies:** New hire onboarding cannot be completed without IT access; delays in equipment or account provisioning directly impact employee productivity and experience

#### Facilities and Security Department
- **Type:** Internal
- **Description:** Internal facilities team providing workspace preparation, building access, and safety services
- **Supplies:** Workspace assignment and setup, building access cards and keys, office furniture and supplies, safety training and equipment, emergency preparedness information, parking permits
- **Service Level Agreement:** Workspace ready by start date, building access card issued within first week, safety training completed within first 5 days
- **Dependencies:** Physical workspace and building access are prerequisites for employee to begin work

#### Finance/Payroll Department
- **Type:** Internal
- **Description:** Internal finance department providing payroll processing and financial system access
- **Supplies:** Payroll setup and processing, first paycheck accuracy, financial system training (for applicable positions), expense reimbursement processing, purchasing card issuance (if applicable)
- **Service Level Agreement:** Payroll setup completed before first pay period, 100% accuracy on first paycheck, payroll training within first 5 days
- **Dependencies:** Accurate and timely payroll is critical to employee satisfaction and legal compliance

### External Suppliers

#### Regional Training Center
- **Type:** External Supplier
- **Description:** Third-party training provider for employee orientation and compliance training programs
- **Supplies:** Compliance training courses (safety, ethics, harassment prevention, accessibility), professional development workshops, specialized technical training, certification programs, leadership development programs
- **Service Level Agreement:** Training courses available on-demand or scheduled monthly, course completion tracking provided, certificates issued upon successful completion
- **Contract Terms:** Annual contract with per-participant pricing, minimum course catalog of 50+ topics, custom content development available
- **Dependencies:** Required compliance training must be completed within 30 days of hire for regulatory compliance

#### Background Check Services Inc.
- **Type:** External Supplier
- **Description:** Third-party background screening provider conducting pre-employment verification
- **Supplies:** Criminal history checks (7-year lookback), employment verification (3 most recent employers), education verification, motor vehicle record checks, professional license verification, drug screening (if required)
- **Service Level Agreement:** Standard background check turnaround 7-10 business days, expedited service available (3-5 days) for critical positions, 99% accuracy guarantee
- **Contract Terms:** Per-check pricing based on screening package, volume discount tiers, online portal access, compliance with Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
- **Dependencies:** Employment offer is contingent upon successful background check clearance; delays impact start date scheduling

#### Benefits Insurance Carriers
- **Type:** External Supplier
- **Description:** Insurance companies providing employee health, dental, vision, life, and disability coverage
- **Supplies:** Insurance plan administration, ID cards, provider networks, claims processing, member services support, online enrollment portals, wellness programs
- **Service Level Agreement:** ID cards mailed within 10 business days of enrollment, claims processed within 30 days, customer service available during business hours
- **Dependencies:** Benefits enrollment and card receipt impact employee satisfaction and ability to access healthcare

#### Illinois Municipal Retirement Fund (IMRF)
- **Type:** External Governmental Entity
- **Description:** State-mandated defined benefit pension system for municipal employees
- **Supplies:** Retirement plan administration, member account management, retirement counseling, beneficiary services, online member portal
- **Service Level Agreement:** New member enrollment within 30 days of hire, annual member statements, retirement estimates available on request
- **Dependencies:** IMRF participation is mandatory for qualifying positions; contribution rates and vesting rules governed by state law

### Technology Systems

**Critical Systems:**

1. **HRIS (BambooHR)**
   - **Purpose:** System of record for all employee data, onboarding workflows, document storage
   - **Dependency:** Core system; onboarding cannot proceed without HRIS access
   - **Integration:** Interfaces with payroll, benefits, time & attendance, applicant tracking
   - **Uptime Requirement:** 99.9% availability during business hours
   - **Vendor:** BambooHR (cloud SaaS)
   - **Risk Mitigation:** Cloud-based with automatic backups, vendor SLA guarantees uptime, offline paper-based backup procedures for critical Day 1 activities

2. **Employee Self-Service Portal**
   - **Purpose:** Pre-employment paperwork completion, document upload, onboarding checklist tracking
   - **Dependency:** New employees must complete portal paperwork 3-5 days before start; portal downtime delays onboarding
   - **Integration:** Connects to HRIS for data synchronization
   - **Uptime Requirement:** 99.5% availability
   - **Risk Mitigation:** Paper forms available as backup, extended deadline flexibility if portal issues occur

3. **Payroll System (Munis)**
   - **Purpose:** Employee payroll processing, tax withholding, deduction management
   - **Dependency:** New employee payroll setup must be completed before first pay period
   - **Integration:** Receives data from HRIS, interfaces with banking system for direct deposit
   - **Criticality:** Payroll errors significantly damage employee experience and trust
   - **Risk Mitigation:** Dual review of all new employee payroll setups, manual paycheck available if system failure

4. **Time & Attendance System (Kronos)**
   - **Purpose:** Electronic timekeeping, time-off requests, timesheet approval
   - **Dependency:** New employees need access by Day 3 for first timesheet entry

   - **Purpose:** Email, calendar, document collaboration, Teams communication
   - **Dependency:** Email account required for all system notifications, training invitations, team collaboration
   - **Uptime Requirement:** 99.9% (vendor SLA)

6. **Learning Management System (LMS)**
   - **Purpose:** Online training delivery, completion tracking, certification management
   - **Dependency:** Required for ethics, compliance, and role-specific training completion

### Change Management

**Service Change Process:**

Any significant changes to the HR Service (policies, processes, technology, service levels) follow this governance process:

1. **Proposal:** Change sponsor (typically HR Manager) documents proposed change including rationale, impact assessment, resource needs, implementation plan
2. **Review:** HR Director reviews and provides input
3. **Stakeholder Input:** Department heads, supervisors, and affected employees consulted for feedback
4. **Approval:** HR Director approves (or escalates to City Manager for major changes impacting budget or policy)
5. **Communication:** Changes communicated clearly to all stakeholders with advance notice
6. **Implementation:** Phased rollout with support resources
7. **Monitoring:** Change impact tracked and course-corrected as needed

### C. System Access Requirements

**All Employees Require:**
- Active Directory user account (network login)
- Email account (Microsoft 365)
- Employee Self-Service Portal access
- HRIS (BambooHR) employee view
- Time & Attendance System (Kronos) - hourly employees for timesheets, salaried for time-off requests
- Intranet portal access
- Microsoft Teams
- Building access card (encoded for assigned buildings/areas)

**Role-Specific Systems:**

**Supervisors/Managers:**
- HRIS supervisor view (view/edit direct reports)
- Time & Attendance approval rights
- Performance management module
- Position budgeting tools (as applicable)

**HR Department:**
- HRIS administrator rights
- Benefits administration portal
- Payroll system (view/edit as per role)
- Background check vendor portal
- Applicant tracking system
- Learning management system administrator
- I-9 compliance system

**Finance/Budget Positions:**
- Munis Financial System (budget, purchasing, accounts payable modules)
- Procurement card (Pcard) if authorized
- Budget reporting tools

**Department-Specific:**
- Permitting and licensing system (Community Development)
- GIS mapping tools (Planning, Public Works)
- Asset management system (Public Works, Fleet)
- Recreation management system (Parks & Recreation)
- Police/Fire dispatch and records systems (Public Safety)
- Library management system (Library)

**Access Provisioning Timeline:**
- Standard systems (email, network, portal): By Day 1
- Role-specific applications: Within first week
- Financial systems requiring enhanced training: Within 2-3 weeks after training completion
- Restricted/sensitive systems: Requires manager approval and security clearance verification

**Security Clearances:**
- Standard background check: All employees
- Enhanced background check: Public Safety, Finance, positions with access to sensitive data
- Fingerprinting: Police, Fire, positions working with vulnerable populations
- Credit check: Finance positions with fiduciary responsibility (with candidate consent)
- Driving record: Positions requiring operation of municipal vehicles
