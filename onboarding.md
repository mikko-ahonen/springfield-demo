# Springfield Municipality - Employee Onboarding Process

## Overview

Welcome to Springfield Municipality! This comprehensive onboarding process ensures all new employees receive proper orientation, training, and integration into our municipal government organization. The process spans from initial job acceptance through the first 90 days of employment and involves multiple departments working together to provide a seamless experience.

Our onboarding philosophy emphasizes compliance, clarity, and connection. New employees will complete all required federal, state, and local documentation, gain access to necessary systems and facilities, understand their role and responsibilities, and build relationships with colleagues and supervisors.

**Timeline:** This process begins when the candidate accepts the job offer and continues through their 90-day probationary period.

**Key Departments Involved:**
- Human Resources Department
- Information Technology Department
- Facilities & Security
- Finance/Payroll Department
- Department-specific teams

---

## Process Steps

### Step 1: Job Offer Acceptance and Background Check Initiation

**Description:** Once the candidate verbally accepts the job offer, HR sends the official offer letter and initiates the background check process. This step confirms the candidate's commitment and begins pre-employment screening required for all municipal employees.

**Timeline:** Day 1 (offer acceptance) - typically 7-14 business days for completion

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** HR Recruiter
- **Accountable:** HR Manager
- **Support:** Background Check Vendor
- **Consulted:** Department Head (for specialized credential verification)
- **Informed:** Hiring Supervisor, Payroll Specialist

**Instructions by Role:**

#### HR Recruiter
1. Prepare the official offer letter including position title, salary/wage, start date, work schedule, benefits summary, and probationary period terms
2. Send offer letter via email with DocuSign for electronic signature
3. Upon receipt of signed offer letter, immediately initiate background check by logging into SpringCheck portal
4. Complete background check request form including: criminal history check (7-year lookback), employment verification (3 most recent employers), education verification (highest degree), motor vehicle record check (if position requires driving), professional license verification (if applicable)
5. Send candidate the background check authorization form via email with instructions to complete within 24 hours
6. Document offer acceptance date and background check initiation date in ATS (ApplicantPro system)
7. Send welcome email to candidate with tentative start date (pending background check clearance)
8. Create new employee folder in shared drive: HR/New Hires/[Year]/[Last Name, First Name]

#### HR Manager
1. Review and approve offer letter before it is sent
2. Monitor background check timeline and escalate any delays beyond 10 business days
3. Review background check results upon completion
4. Make final hiring decision if any flags appear on background check
5. Authorize HR Recruiter to proceed with onboarding once background check is cleared

#### Hiring Supervisor
1. Respond to background check vendor within 24 hours if contacted for employment verification
2. Review welcome email template and provide any department-specific information to add

#### Payroll Specialist
1. Review offer letter to ensure salary/wage information is accurate and within approved budget
2. Note anticipated start date in payroll calendar for processing timeline

### Step 2: Pre-Boarding Preparation

**Description:** After successful background check clearance, HR coordinates with IT, Facilities, and the hiring department to prepare workspace, technology, and access credentials for the new employee's first day.

**Timeline:** 5-7 business days before start date

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** HR Coordinator
- **Accountable:** HR Manager
- **Support:** IT Administrator, Facilities Coordinator, Department Administrative Assistant
- **Consulted:** Hiring Supervisor
- **Informed:** Department Head, Payroll Specialist

**Instructions by Role:**

#### HR Coordinator
1. Confirm official start date with new employee via phone call and email
2. Send pre-boarding email packet including: first day arrival time and location, parking instructions and temporary parking pass, dress code policy, what to bring (photo ID, voided check for direct deposit, I-9 documents), new hire paperwork portal link, benefits orientation schedule
3. Create new employee record in HRIS (BambooHR) with all known information
4. Submit IT Access Request form including: position title, department, supervisor name, start date, equipment needs (laptop/desktop, monitor, phone), software/system access requirements
5. Submit Facilities Request form for: office/workstation assignment, building access card, keys (if needed), nameplate/door sign
6. Schedule new hire orientation session (half-day HR orientation on Day 1 or 2)
7. Assign employee ID number from HR sequence log
8. Create email distribution to all relevant parties with new hire start date and preparation checklist
9. Prepare new hire orientation packet (physical folder) containing: welcome letter, organizational chart, employee handbook, benefits summary, emergency procedures, forms requiring wet signatures

#### IT Administrator
1. Upon receipt of IT Access Request, create user account in Active Directory with username format: firstinitiallastname@springfield.gov
2. Generate temporary password and send securely to HR Coordinator
3. Provision email account in Microsoft 365
4. Create network drive access for employee's department
5. Order or prepare computer equipment (desktop or laptop as specified)
6. Install standard software suite: Microsoft Office 365, Acrobat Reader, Chrome browser, department-specific applications
7. Configure desk phone or VoIP extension
8. Prepare equipment setup appointment for Day 1 (30-minute session)
9. Add new employee to IT orientation calendar for system training (Week 1)
10. Update IT inventory spreadsheet with assigned equipment serial numbers

#### Facilities Coordinator
1. Upon receipt of Facilities Request, assign available workstation or office space
2. Inspect workspace for cleanliness and functionality (working lights, outlets, furniture)
3. Coordinate with janitorial staff for deep cleaning if needed
4. Prepare building access card (encode with appropriate access levels based on position)
5. Prepare keys if the employee needs access to locked rooms, cabinets, or buildings
6. Create temporary visitor parking pass valid for first week
7. Order nameplate for desk/door (5-7 business day lead time)
8. Stock workspace with basic supplies: pens, notepad, stapler, tape, paper clips
9. Place welcome packet on desk the day before employee starts
10. Update facilities seating chart and emergency contact list

#### Hiring Supervisor
1. Confirm workstation location is appropriate for team collaboration
2. Identify an onboarding buddy (peer mentor) for the new employee
3. Prepare first-week schedule including: meetings with supervisor, team introductions, initial assignments, training sessions
4. Notify team members of new hire start date and role
5. Plan team welcome lunch for end of first week
6. Review department-specific orientation topics to cover
7. Prepare list of required department-specific systems/tools for IT access

#### Department Administrative Assistant
1. Add new employee to department phone list and email distribution groups
2. Order business cards (if applicable for position)
3. Set up department-specific access (shared calendars, project management tools, departmental drives)
4. Prepare department welcome packet with team contact information
5. Coordinate with supervisor on first-week training schedule

### Step 3: New Hire Paperwork Portal Completion

**Description:** New employee completes mandatory employment forms online through the HR portal before their first day, reducing first-day paperwork burden and allowing HR to prepare documents in advance.

**Timeline:** 3-5 business days before start date

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** New Employee
- **Accountable:** HR Coordinator
- **Support:** Payroll Specialist
- **Consulted:** Benefits Administrator
- **Informed:** Hiring Supervisor

**Instructions by Role:**

#### HR Coordinator
1. Send new hire portal login credentials via email with subject line: "Action Required: Springfield Municipality Pre-Employment Forms"
2. Include video tutorial link showing how to complete each form
3. Provide HR contact information for questions: HR helpline (555-0100) and email (hr@springfield.gov)
4. Monitor portal completion status daily
5. Send reminder email if not completed within 48 hours
6. Review completed forms for accuracy and completeness
7. Follow up on any missing information or errors
8. Print completed forms that require wet signatures for Day 1 meeting
9. Forward emergency contact information to supervisor
10. Route completed documents to appropriate departments (payroll, benefits, etc.)

#### New Employee
1. Access new hire portal using credentials provided by HR
2. Complete all required forms in the portal:
   - **Personal Information Form:** Legal name, preferred name, date of birth, Social Security number, personal email, phone number, home address
   - **Emergency Contact Form:** List at least 2 emergency contacts with names, relationships, phone numbers, and addresses
   - **Direct Deposit Authorization:** Bank name, routing number, account number (upload voided check image)
   - **W-4 Federal Tax Withholding:** Complete current year W-4 form
   - **State Tax Withholding Form:** Complete IL-W-4 for Illinois withholding
   - **Benefits Acknowledgment:** Acknowledge receipt of benefits information and confirm attendance at benefits orientation
   - **Employee Handbook Acknowledgment:** Affirm you will read the handbook and agree to policies
   - **IT Acceptable Use Policy:** Read and electronically sign acceptable use agreement
   - **Ethics and Conflict of Interest Statement:** Disclose any potential conflicts of interest
   - **Confidentiality Agreement:** Acknowledge handling of sensitive municipal information
3. Upload required documents: photo ID (driver's license or state ID), I-9 documentation (see I-9 acceptable documents list), voided check for direct deposit, professional licenses or certifications (if applicable)
4. Review all information carefully before submitting
5. Submit portal forms and save confirmation email
6. Prepare original documents to bring on first day for I-9 verification

#### Payroll Specialist
1. Review completed W-4, state tax withholding, and direct deposit forms
2. Verify direct deposit information matches uploaded voided check
3. Set up new employee in payroll system (Munis Payroll module)
4. Enter employee ID number, hire date, salary/wage, pay frequency, tax withholding elections, and direct deposit information
5. Assign to correct department cost center and grant/fund if applicable
6. Schedule payroll processing based on hire date and pay period calendar
7. Flag any discrepancies or missing information and notify HR Coordinator
8. Prepare first paycheck timeline communication for HR to share with new employee

#### Benefits Administrator
1. Review benefits acknowledgment and note enrollment meeting attendance
2. Prepare benefits enrollment packet for first-day meeting
3. Access employee information in benefits portal to pre-populate enrollment forms
4. Confirm employee eligibility date based on hire date and collective bargaining agreement (if applicable)

### Step 4: First Day - Morning Arrival and Welcome

**Description:** New employee arrives at Springfield Municipality for their first day. HR greets them, conducts I-9 verification, completes remaining paperwork, and provides initial orientation to the organization.

**Timeline:** Day 1, 8:00 AM - 12:00 PM

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** HR Coordinator
- **Accountable:** HR Manager
- **Support:** Benefits Administrator, IT Administrator
- **Consulted:** N/A
- **Informed:** Hiring Supervisor, Department Head

**Instructions by Role:**

#### HR Coordinator
1. Arrive 15 minutes early to prepare conference room for new hire orientation
2. Set up orientation materials: employee handbook, forms folder, benefits packet, welcome folder, computer equipment (if provided by HR)
3. Greet new employee at main lobby reception at 8:00 AM
4. Escort to HR conference room and offer coffee/water
5. Conduct warm welcome and overview of first day schedule
6. Complete I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification:
   - Review Section 1 completed by employee in portal
   - Examine original documents from List A (passport or passport card) OR List B (driver's license or state ID) + List C (Social Security card or birth certificate)
   - Photocopy documents for I-9 file
   - Complete Section 2 of I-9 form with document details
   - Sign and date I-9 form
   - File I-9 in secure cabinet (separate from personnel file)
7. Review and collect wet signatures on printed forms:
   - Employee handbook acknowledgment
   - IT acceptable use policy
   - Confidentiality agreement
   - Ethics statement
   - Direct deposit authorization (if not signed electronically)
8. Conduct HR orientation presentation (60-90 minutes) covering:
   - Welcome from HR Manager (video or in-person)
   - Springfield Municipality mission, vision, and values
   - Organizational structure and department overview
   - Employee handbook highlights (leave policies, attendance, conduct expectations, dress code)
   - Probationary period expectations (90 days)
   - Performance evaluation process
   - Payroll schedule and timekeeping procedures
   - Workplace safety and emergency procedures
   - Anti-discrimination and harassment policies
   - Whistleblower policy and reporting procedures
9. Issue temporary building access badge
10. Take employee photo for permanent badge and employee directory
11. Provide employee ID number and explain its use
12. Answer questions
13. Escort employee to department and introduce to supervisor (before noon)
14. Send "Day 1 Completed" email to supervisor confirming new hire arrival

#### HR Manager
1. Stop by orientation to personally welcome new employee (15-minute meet and greet)
2. Share enthusiasm about their joining the team
3. Offer to answer any questions about Springfield Municipality
4. Provide business card and encourage open-door policy
5. Sign welcome letter in employee orientation packet

#### Benefits Administrator
1. Join orientation session for 30-minute benefits overview presentation covering:
   - Health insurance options (PPO, HMO, HDHP plans)
   - Dental and vision insurance
   - Life insurance and AD&D
   - Retirement plan (IMRF - Illinois Municipal Retirement Fund)
   - Deferred compensation (457 plan) options
   - Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA) for healthcare and dependent care
   - Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
   - Paid time off (vacation, sick leave, holidays)
   - Enrollment deadlines (30 days from hire date)
   - Benefits portal access and navigation
2. Schedule one-on-one benefits enrollment meeting (within first week)
3. Provide benefits enrollment packet with rate sheets and plan comparison charts
4. Answer initial questions about coverage and costs
5. Provide contact information for follow-up questions

#### IT Administrator
1. Meet new employee at 11:30 AM (or after HR orientation concludes)
2. Provide computer equipment (laptop or desktop), monitor, keyboard, mouse, and peripherals
3. Assist with initial setup and login to workstation
4. Provide username and temporary password
5. Walk through password change process (must change on first login)
6. Configure desk phone or demonstrate VoIP phone app
7. Test phone by placing a call
8. Confirm email access in Outlook
9. Provide IT Welcome Guide with instructions for:
   - Accessing network drives
   - Connecting to Wi-Fi
   - Remote access/VPN setup
   - Password reset procedures
   - IT help desk contact information (helpdesk@springfield.gov or extension 5000)
10. Schedule comprehensive IT orientation for Day 2 or 3
11. Add employee phone extension to municipal directory

### Step 5: First Day - Department Orientation and Workspace Setup

**Description:** Supervisor welcomes new employee to the department, conducts facility tour, introduces team members, and helps them settle into their workspace.

**Timeline:** Day 1, 12:00 PM - 5:00 PM

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** Hiring Supervisor
- **Accountable:** Department Head
- **Support:** Onboarding Buddy, Department Administrative Assistant
- **Consulted:** N/A
- **Informed:** Department Team Members

**Instructions by Role:**

#### Hiring Supervisor
1. Meet new employee at HR department at noon or after HR orientation concludes
2. Welcome employee to the department with enthusiasm
3. Escort to assigned workspace and allow time to settle in
4. Verify workspace has necessary equipment and supplies
5. Provide building tour (30-45 minutes) including:
   - Restrooms
   - Break rooms and kitchens
   - Copy/print rooms
   - Supply closets
   - Conference rooms
   - Emergency exits and fire extinguisher locations
   - Mailroom
   - Parking areas
   - Security office
6. Introduce new employee to team members individually or in small groups
7. Formally introduce onboarding buddy and explain buddy role
8. Conduct department orientation discussion covering:
   - Department mission and key responsibilities
   - Team structure and reporting relationships
   - Department norms and culture
   - Communication preferences (email, in-person, chat)
   - Meeting schedules (team meetings, one-on-ones, all-staff)
   - Department-specific policies or procedures
9. Review position responsibilities and initial priorities
10. Provide overview of key projects or initiatives
11. Discuss first week expectations and training plan
12. Schedule daily check-in meetings for first week (15-30 minutes each morning)
13. Schedule weekly one-on-one meetings going forward
14. Answer questions and provide reassurance
15. Allow time for employee to organize workspace and review materials
16. Check in at end of day to see how first day went

#### Department Head
1. Stop by new employee's workspace mid-afternoon for informal welcome (10-15 minutes)
2. Express excitement about them joining the department
3. Share department priorities and goals
4. Explain your role and accessibility
5. Encourage questions anytime
6. Invite to department team meeting

#### Onboarding Buddy
1. Meet new employee during department introductions
2. Exchange contact information (phone, email)
3. Offer to answer questions anytime, especially informal or cultural questions
4. Schedule coffee/lunch for Day 2 or 3 to get acquainted
5. Share insider tips about: best places for lunch nearby, parking tips, navigating the building, department culture and unwritten rules, who to go to for different types of questions
6. Check in daily during first week via email or in-person
7. Proactively introduce new employee to other colleagues
8. Invite to join for breaks or lunch

#### Department Administrative Assistant
1. Add new employee to department calendar and email distribution lists
2. Set up recurring meetings (team meetings, staff meetings, etc.)
3. Provide department roster with names, titles, phone extensions, and email addresses
4. Show how to reserve conference rooms and request supplies
5. Explain department filing system and shared drives
6. Provide copy/print codes if needed
7. Share department contact list and organizational chart

### Step 6: IT Systems Training

**Description:** IT provides comprehensive training on all technology systems, software applications, and tools the employee needs to perform their job effectively.

**Timeline:** Day 2-3

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** IT Administrator
- **Accountable:** IT Director
- **Support:** Department System Administrator (if specialized systems)
- **Consulted:** Hiring Supervisor
- **Informed:** HR Coordinator

**Instructions by Role:**

#### IT Administrator
1. Schedule 2-hour IT orientation session in IT training room
2. Prepare training agenda customized to employee's role and system access needs
3. Conduct hands-on training covering:
   - **Email and Calendar (Microsoft Outlook):**
     - Sending/receiving email
     - Calendar management and scheduling meetings
     - Shared calendars
     - Out-of-office replies
     - Email signature setup
   - **Network Drives:**
     - Accessing department shared drives
     - Personal drive for file storage
     - Folder structure and organization
     - File permissions and sharing
   - **Intranet Portal:**
     - Accessing employee portal
     - Finding policies and forms
     - Submitting help desk tickets
     - Viewing municipal announcements
   - **Time and Attendance System:**
     - Logging in to timesheet system (Kronos)
     - Entering hours worked
     - Submitting timesheets for approval
     - Requesting time off
   - **Microsoft Teams:**
     - Setting up profile
     - Joining channels
     - Chat and video calling
     - Document collaboration
   - **Municipal-Specific Applications:**
     - Provide training on role-specific software (permitting system, GIS, financial system, etc.)
     - Create login credentials for specialized systems
     - Share user guides and resources
4. Demonstrate password management and security best practices:
   - Creating strong passwords
   - Password expiration policy (every 90 days)
   - Multi-factor authentication setup
   - Recognizing phishing attempts
5. Review IT policies:
   - Acceptable use of technology
   - Data security and confidentiality
   - Personal use of municipal devices
   - Software installation restrictions
   - BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) policy
6. Provide contact information for IT support:
   - Help desk phone: extension 5000
   - Help desk email: helpdesk@springfield.gov
   - Self-service portal for password resets
   - IT staff directory
7. Demonstrate how to submit help desk tickets
8. Provide IT Quick Reference Guide (printed and electronic)
9. Set up remote access/VPN if employee will work remotely
10. Test VPN connection and remote desktop access
11. Answer questions and provide time for practice
12. Follow up via email with summary and links to resources

#### IT Director
1. Approve specialized software licenses or access requests
2. Review security access levels for sensitive systems
3. Ensure IT Administrator has sufficient time allocated for training
4. Follow up with IT Administrator on training completion

#### Department System Administrator (if applicable)
1. Provide supplementary training on department-specific applications
2. Create user accounts in specialized systems (e.g., permitting software, asset management, GIS)
3. Assign appropriate permission levels based on role
4. Share department-specific technical documentation
5. Offer ongoing support for technical questions

#### Hiring Supervisor
1. Review IT training agenda to ensure it covers all systems employee needs
2. Identify any additional software or tools not on standard list
3. Request additional training sessions if needed

### Step 7: Benefits Enrollment

**Description:** Benefits Administrator meets one-on-one with new employee to review all benefit options in detail and assist with enrollment selections.

**Timeline:** Within first week (by Day 5)

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** Benefits Administrator
- **Accountable:** HR Manager
- **Support:** Insurance Broker Representative (available for questions)
- **Consulted:** N/A
- **Informed:** Payroll Specialist

**Instructions by Role:**

#### Benefits Administrator
1. Schedule 60-90 minute one-on-one benefits enrollment meeting in private conference room
2. Bring comprehensive benefits materials: plan comparison charts, rate sheets, provider directories, summary plan descriptions, enrollment forms
3. Review employee's family situation to determine coverage needs
4. Provide detailed explanation of each benefit:
   - **Health Insurance:**
     - Explain PPO, HMO, and HDHP plan differences
     - Review monthly premiums for employee, employee + spouse, employee + children, family
     - Explain deductibles, copays, coinsurance, and out-of-pocket maximums
     - Show provider network directories
     - Discuss HSA eligibility with HDHP
   - **Dental Insurance:**
     - Review coverage levels (preventive, basic, major)
     - Explain premium costs
     - Show dentist network
   - **Vision Insurance:**
     - Explain exam, frames, and lens coverage
     - Review premium costs
   - **Life Insurance:**
     - Explain employer-paid basic life insurance (1x salary)
     - Offer voluntary supplemental life insurance options
     - Discuss AD&D coverage
   - **Disability Insurance:**
     - Review short-term and long-term disability coverage
     - Explain benefit amounts and waiting periods
   - **Retirement Plan (IMRF):**
     - Explain defined benefit pension plan
     - Review employee contribution rates (tiered based on hire date)
     - Discuss vesting schedule
     - Provide estimated retirement benefit calculator
     - Explain beneficiary designation
   - **Deferred Compensation (457 Plan):**
     - Explain voluntary retirement savings plan
     - Discuss contribution limits and tax advantages
     - Review investment options
     - Provide enrollment materials from plan provider
   - **Flexible Spending Accounts:**
     - Explain healthcare FSA (use-it-or-lose-it provision)
     - Explain dependent care FSA (day care, after-school care)
     - Discuss contribution limits and eligible expenses
   - **Employee Assistance Program:**
     - Explain free counseling services (6 sessions per year)
     - Discuss confidentiality
     - Provide EAP contact information
   - **Paid Time Off:**
     - Review vacation accrual rates based on years of service
     - Explain sick leave accrual
     - List paid holidays (11 per year)
     - Discuss PTO request procedures
5. Help employee make informed decisions based on their needs and budget
6. Complete enrollment forms together:
   - Medical, dental, and vision elections
   - Life insurance beneficiary designation
   - Disability coverage
   - FSA contribution elections
7. Review completed forms for accuracy
8. Calculate total benefit deductions and show impact on take-home pay
9. Provide summary of elections for employee records
10. Submit enrollment forms to insurance carriers within 24 hours
11. Provide benefits ID cards timeline (2-3 weeks)
12. Schedule 30-day follow-up to ensure cards received and answer questions
13. Send confirmation email with summary of elections and effective dates

#### HR Manager
1. Review complex benefit situations or exceptions
2. Approve benefit elections outside standard enrollment
3. Address escalated benefits questions

#### Payroll Specialist
1. Receive copy of completed enrollment forms from Benefits Administrator
2. Enter benefit deductions into payroll system
3. Verify deduction amounts match rate sheets
4. Ensure deductions begin in first paycheck (or as specified by plan rules)
5. Monitor for processing errors

### Step 8: Safety and Emergency Procedures Training

**Description:** Facilities and Safety Coordinator provides training on workplace safety protocols, emergency procedures, and building-specific safety information.

**Timeline:** Day 3-5

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** Safety Coordinator
- **Accountable:** Facilities Manager
- **Support:** HR Coordinator
- **Consulted:** Department Safety Representative
- **Informed:** Hiring Supervisor

**Instructions by Role:**

#### Safety Coordinator
1. Schedule 45-60 minute safety orientation session
2. Conduct workplace safety training covering:
   - **Emergency Procedures:**
     - Fire alarm procedures and evacuation routes
     - Designated assembly point locations
     - Severe weather procedures (tornado, earthquake)
     - Active threat/lockdown procedures
     - Medical emergency response
   - **Building Safety:**
     - Emergency exit locations
     - Fire extinguisher locations and use
     - AED (Automated External Defibrillator) locations
     - First aid kit locations
     - Emergency call boxes
   - **Workplace Safety:**
     - Slip, trip, and fall prevention
     - Ergonomics and workstation setup
     - Proper lifting techniques
     - Reporting unsafe conditions
   - **Incident Reporting:**
     - How to report workplace injuries
     - Workers' compensation process
     - Incident report forms
     - Reporting timelines (within 24 hours)
   - **Security:**
     - Building access and badge procedures
     - Visitor sign-in requirements
     - After-hours access procedures
     - Lost badge reporting
     - Suspicious activity reporting
3. Conduct walking tour of emergency equipment and exits
4. Demonstrate how to use fire extinguisher (hands-on if training extinguisher available)
5. Review department-specific safety considerations
6. Provide emergency contact card for wallet
7. Distribute safety manual or reference guide
8. Require employee to sign safety training acknowledgment form
9. Answer safety-related questions
10. File completed training form in employee's HR file
11. Add employee to emergency contact list and evacuation roster
12. Schedule annual safety refresher training

#### Facilities Manager
1. Ensure Safety Coordinator has adequate time for training
2. Review and approve safety training materials annually
3. Maintain records of safety training completion
4. Coordinate emergency drills and exercises

#### Department Safety Representative
1. Introduce yourself as department safety contact
2. Explain department-specific safety protocols or hazards
3. Show location of department first aid kit and safety equipment
4. Encourage reporting of safety concerns

#### Hiring Supervisor
1. Reinforce safety expectations in daily work
2. Ensure employee understands department-specific safety rules
3. Model safe work practices

### Step 10: Ethics and Compliance Training

**Description:** New employee completes mandatory ethics training covering public sector ethics, conflicts of interest, code of conduct, and compliance with municipal policies and state laws.

**Timeline:** Week 1

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** New Employee
- **Accountable:** Ethics Officer (City Attorney)
- **Support:** HR Coordinator
- **Consulted:** N/A
- **Informed:** Hiring Supervisor, HR Manager

**Instructions by Role:**

#### New Employee
1. Access ethics training module via learning management system (assigned automatically upon hire)
2. Complete online ethics training (approximately 60 minutes)
3. Training topics include:
   - Springfield Municipality Code of Conduct
   - Illinois Public Officer Prohibited Activities Act
   - Conflicts of interest identification and disclosure
   - Gift ban policies (prohibited from accepting gifts from vendors, contractors, lobbyists)
   - Use of municipal resources for personal purposes (prohibited)
   - Confidentiality of municipal information
   - Social media policies for public employees
   - Political activity restrictions (Hatch Act for federally funded positions)
   - Whistleblower protections and reporting procedures
   - Anti-discrimination and equal employment opportunity
   - Harassment prevention
   - Retaliation prohibition
4. Review case study scenarios and answer knowledge check questions
5. Pass final quiz with 80% or higher score (may retake if needed)
6. Electronically sign ethics acknowledgment statement
7. Print certificate of completion
8. Submit certificate to HR Coordinator
9. Contact Ethics Officer if you have questions about specific situations

#### Ethics Officer (City Attorney)
1. Develop and maintain ethics training content
2. Update training annually to reflect law changes
3. Review ethics training completion reports monthly
4. Provide guidance on ethics questions or situations
5. Conduct in-person ethics training for senior staff and department heads

### Step 30: 90-Day Onboarding Completion and Feedback

**Description:** HR collects comprehensive feedback from new employee about onboarding experience, identifies improvement opportunities, and celebrates successful completion of onboarding program.

**Timeline:** Day 90

**RASCI:**
- **Responsible:** HR Business Partner
- **Accountable:** HR Manager
- **Support:** HR Coordinator
- **Consulted:** New Employee, Hiring Supervisor
- **Informed:** Department Head, HR Director

**Instructions by Role:**

#### HR Business Partner
1. Send 90-day onboarding survey to employee via email with link to anonymous online survey
2. Survey topics include:
   - **Pre-boarding Experience:**
     - Clarity of communication before start date
     - Preparedness for first day
     - Ease of completing paperwork
   - **First Day:**
     - Quality of welcome and orientation
     - Effectiveness of HR orientation
     - Workspace readiness
   - **Training and Development:**
     - Adequacy of job training
     - Quality of IT training
     - Usefulness of various training sessions
     - Training gaps or needs
   - **Support and Resources:**
     - Supervisor support and availability
     - Onboarding buddy effectiveness
     - HR responsiveness
     - Availability of tools and resources
   - **Integration:**
     - Feeling welcome and included on team
     - Understanding of role and expectations
     - Confidence in ability to perform job
     - Connection to organizational mission
   - **Overall Experience:**
     - Overall satisfaction with onboarding (1-5 scale)
     - What went well
     - What could be improved
     - Suggestions for future new hires
     - Likelihood to recommend Springfield Municipality as employer (NPS score)
3. Schedule 30-minute one-on-one debrief conversation (optional but encouraged)
4. Discuss survey responses and gather additional insights
5. Ask open-ended questions:
   - "What was the most helpful part of onboarding?"
   - "What was missing or could have been better?"
   - "How prepared do you feel to succeed in your role?"
   - "What advice would you give to improve onboarding?"
6. Thank employee for feedback
7. Explain how feedback will be used to improve process
8. Compile survey data and themes across all new hires
9. Prepare quarterly onboarding report for HR leadership including:
   - Survey results and trends
   - Common themes from feedback
   - Specific improvement recommendations
   - Success stories and best practices
   - Onboarding completion rates
   - 90-day retention rates
10. Present findings to HR team and recommend process improvements
11. Share relevant feedback with supervisors and departments (anonymized)
12. Implement improvements based on feedback

#### HR Manager
1. Review onboarding survey results and identify trends
2. Prioritize improvement initiatives
3. Allocate resources for onboarding enhancements
4. Report onboarding metrics to senior leadership
5. Recognize supervisors and departments with exceptional onboarding

#### HR Coordinator
1. Monitor survey completion rates
2. Send reminder to employees who haven't completed survey
3. Compile survey data and create summary reports
4. Update onboarding materials based on feedback

#### Hiring Supervisor
1. Participate in feedback discussion if requested
2. Receive feedback summary from HR
3. Reflect on onboarding effectiveness
4. Implement improvements for future new hires

#### New Employee
1. Complete 90-day onboarding survey honestly and thoughtfully
2. Provide constructive feedback to help improve process
3. Participate in debrief conversation with HR (optional)
4. Share what worked well and what could be better
5. Offer ideas for improvement
